> **Q:** What is the difference between a lumpectomy and mastectomy?

**Answer:**
> ### Understanding Lumpectomy and Mastectomy
>
> **Lumpectomy (Breast-Conserving Surgery):**
> - Removes only the tumor and a small margin of surrounding tissue
> - Preserves most of the breast
> - Usually followed by radiation therapy
> - Often suitable for early-stage cancers
>
> **Mastectomy:**
> - Removes the entire breast tissue
> - May be recommended for larger tumors or multiple areas of cancer
> - Several types available (simple, skin-sparing, nipple-sparing)
> - Reconstruction options available
>
> Both approaches can be equally effective for treating breast cancer. Your healthcare team will recommend the best option based on your specific situation.

>
> ### Radiation and Hair Loss:
> - Radiation therapy only affects hair in the **treated area**
